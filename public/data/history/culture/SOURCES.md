# Sources: art-movements.csv

## Reference Works

- **Grove Art Online / Oxford Art Online** — authoritative dates for movement periods, canonical start/end conventions
- **H.H. Arnason & Elizabeth C. Mansfield, *A History of Modern Art*** (7th ed.) — Impressionism through Postmodernism
- **E.H. Gombrich, *The Story of Art*** (Pocket ed.) — broad sweep from antiquity through the 20th century
- **Janson's *History of Art*** (8th ed.) — standard academic reference for all periods
- **Encyclopaedia Britannica** online — individual movement articles cross-checked for dates

## Per-Entry Notes

### Greek Classical Period (-480 to -323)
Start: Battle of Salamis (480 BCE), conventionally marks the Classical period onset. End: Death of Alexander the Great (323 BCE).

### Hellenistic Art (-323 to -31)
Start: Death of Alexander the Great. End: Battle of Actium (31 BCE) and Roman absorption of Ptolemaic Egypt.

### Roman Art (-509 to 476)
Start: traditional founding of the Roman Republic (509 BCE). End: fall of the Western Roman Empire (476 CE).

### Mauryan Art (-322 to -185)
Start: founding of the Maurya Empire by Chandragupta Maurya (322 BCE). End: assassination of Brihadratha (185 BCE).

### Gupta Sculpture (320 to 550)
Start: founding of the Gupta Empire under Chandragupta I (~320 CE). End: collapse under Huna invasions (~550 CE).

### Byzantine Art (330 to 1453)
Start: dedication of Constantinople (330 CE). End: Ottoman conquest of Constantinople (1453 CE).

### Tang Dynasty Art (618 to 907)
Start/end: Tang Dynasty dates.

### Islamic Golden Age (750 to 1258)
Start: Abbasid Caliphate (~750 CE). End: Mongol sack of Baghdad (1258 CE).

### Carolingian Art (750 to 900)
Start: Carolingian dynasty consolidation (~750 CE). End: dissolution of the Carolingian Empire (~900 CE).

### Heian Period Culture (794 to 1185)
Start: transfer of capital to Heian-kyo (794 CE). End: Genpei War and rise of the Kamakura shogunate (1185 CE).

### Song Dynasty Landscape Painting (960 to 1279)
Start/end: Song Dynasty dates.

### Romanesque Art (1000 to 1150)
Approximate period of Romanesque architectural dominance in Western Europe.

### Medieval Islamic Architecture (1000 to 1500)
Broad span covering major mosque and madrasa construction across the Islamic world.

### Gothic Art (1150 to 1400)
Start: Abbot Suger's rebuilding of Saint-Denis (~1140s). End: transitional to Early Renaissance in Italy (~1400).

### Indian Temple Sculpture (400 to 1000)
Covers Gupta-period beginnings through Chola and Pallava temple traditions.

### Aztec Art (1300 to 1521)
Start: founding of Tenochtitlan (~1300 CE). End: Spanish conquest (1521 CE).

### Italian Renaissance (1400 to 1600)
Start: conventional academic dating beginning in Florence (~1400). End: broadly considered over by ~1600 with the rise of Baroque. *Note: some scholars date the end to the Sack of Rome (1527) for the High Renaissance specifically.*

### Northern Renaissance (1430 to 1580)
Start: Jan van Eyck's mature works and Flemish panel painting (~1430s). End: transition to Baroque in northern Europe.

### High Renaissance (1490 to 1527)
Start: Leonardo's mature period (~1490s). End: Sack of Rome (1527), which disrupted the Roman artistic community.

### Renaissance Polyphony (1400 to 1600)
Corresponds broadly to the Franco-Flemish school and the Renaissance in music.

### Elizabethan Theatre (1558 to 1642)
Start: accession of Elizabeth I (1558). End: closing of the theatres by Parliament (1642).

### Mannerism (1520 to 1600)
Start: follows the High Renaissance (~1520). End: absorbed into early Baroque (~1600).

### Baroque (1600 to 1750)
Start: Rome, early 17th century. End: death of J.S. Bach (1750) often cited as the conventional close.

### Baroque Music (1600 to 1750)
Same conventional boundaries as visual Baroque.

### Mughal Painting (1600 to 1857)
Start: Akbar's atelier (~1600). End: dissolution of the Mughal Empire (1857).

### Edo Period Culture (1603 to 1868)
Start/end: Edo (Tokugawa) period dates.

### Classicism (1620 to 1800)
Covers French Academic classicism under the Académie royale through the 18th century.

### Rococo (1720 to 1780)
Start: Paris salons and Watteau's fêtes galantes (~1720). End: supplanted by Neoclassicism (~1780).

### Classical Period Music (1750 to 1820)
Start: mid-18th century shift from Baroque; Haydn and Mozart. End: Beethoven's late period and transition to Romanticism.

### Neoclassicism (1760 to 1850)
Start: reaction to Rococo, Winckelmann's writings (~1755-1764). End: absorbed into Romanticism (~1850).

### Romanticism (1800 to 1850)
Start: approximate, early 19th century (Géricault, Delacroix). End: mid-century transition to Realism.

### Romantic Music (1820 to 1900)
Start: Schubert, Schumann. End: late Romantic composers (Brahms, Mahler, early 20th century).

### Realism (1840 to 1880)
Start: Courbet and the Barbizon school (~1840s). End: overlap with Impressionism and Naturalism.

### Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood (1848 to 1880)
Start: founding of the Brotherhood (1848). End: dissolution of the group and transition to Aestheticism.

### Impressionism (1860 to 1900)
Start: corrected from 1865 to 1860. The movement is typically dated from the early 1860s (Manet's *Le Déjeuner sur l'herbe* was 1863; the first Impressionist Exhibition was 1874). Monet's *Impression, Sunrise* (1872) gave the movement its name retroactively. End: ~1900, transition to Post-Impressionism.
**Correction:** start_year was 1865, corrected to 1860.

### Art Nouveau (1890 to 1910)
Start: Paris Exposition Universelle (1889) and early 1890s proliferation. End: World War I.

### Post-Impressionism (1886 to 1910)
Start: Seurat's *A Sunday on La Grande Jatte* exhibited 1886. End: Cubism and Expressionism emerge.

### Symbolism (1886 to 1910)
Start: Jean Moréas' Symbolist Manifesto (1886). End: absorbed into early modernism.

### Expressionism (1905 to 1930)
Start: Die Brücke founded (1905). End: dissolution under Nazi suppression and the rise of later modernisms.

### Fauvism (1905 to 1910)
Start: Salon d'Automne (1905). End: brief movement, dissolved by ~1910.

### Cubism (1907 to 1935)
Start: Picasso's *Les Demoiselles d'Avignon* (1907). End: late Synthetic Cubism and its descendants into the 1930s.

### Futurism (1909 to 1944)
Start: Marinetti's Futurist Manifesto (1909). End: death of Marinetti (1944).

### Constructivism (1913 to 1940)
Start: Vladimir Tatlin's early constructions (~1913). End: suppressed under Socialist Realism.

### Dada (1916 to 1924)
Start: Cabaret Voltaire, Zurich (1916). End: First Surrealist Manifesto effectively absorbed Dada (1924).
**Correction:** end_year was 1923, corrected to 1924.

### Bauhaus (1919 to 1933)
Start: founding by Walter Gropius in Weimar (1919). End: closure under Nazi pressure (1933).

### Surrealism (1924 to 1966)
Start: André Breton's First Surrealist Manifesto (1924). End: Breton's death (1966) is often cited as the close of the official movement.
**Correction:** start_year was 1920, corrected to 1924; end_year was 1950, corrected to 1966.

### Latin American Muralism (1920 to 1960)
Start: Mexican muralist movement post-revolution (~1920). End: decline of the major commissions by ~1960.

### Jazz Age (1920 to 1940)
Start: post-WWI emergence of jazz in the United States. End: swing era and WWII transition.

### Abstract Expressionism (1940 to 1965)
Start: New York School emerges (~1940s). End: Pop Art and Minimalism supersede it (~1965).

### Pop Art (1955 to 1975)
Start: Richard Hamilton and Eduardo Paolozzi in the UK (~1955); Andy Warhol and Roy Lichtenstein in the US from ~1960. End: transitions to Postmodernism (~1975).

### Minimalism (1960 to 1980)
Start: Donald Judd, Dan Flavin, Carl Andre (~1960). End: overlaps with Postmodernism.

### Postmodernism (1970 to 2000)
Approximate span of postmodern dominance in Western art criticism and practice.

### Hip-Hop Culture (1973 to present)
Start: DJ Kool Herc's block party, South Bronx (August 11, 1973) — the conventional founding moment.

### Street Art (1980 to present)
Start: approximate mainstream emergence; graffiti culture had earlier roots but street art as a distinct genre is generally dated from the early 1980s.

### Digital Art (1990 to present)
Start: personal computing and early internet art (~1990). Earlier computer art existed (1960s-1980s) but 1990 marks broad accessibility.
