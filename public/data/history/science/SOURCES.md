## Per-Entry Notes

### Euclidean Geometry (-300)
Euclid's *Elements* is conventionally dated to ~300 BCE. Exact date unknown; -300 is the standard approximation.

### Archimedes' Principles (-250)
Archimedes active ~287–212 BCE. His key works (lever, buoyancy) are approximately dated ~250 BCE.

### Measurement of Earth's Circumference (-240)
Eratosthenes' measurement is traditionally dated to ~240 BCE.

### Ptolemy's Almagest (150)
The *Almagest* was composed ~150 CE.

### Hindu-Arabic Numeral System (628)
Brahmagupta's *Brahmasphutasiddhanta* (628 CE) is the key codification of positional notation with zero.

### Al-Khwarizmi's Algebra (830)
*Al-Kitab al-mukhtasar fi hisab al-jabr wal-muqabala* composed ~830 CE.

### Ibn al-Haytham's Book of Optics (1021)
*Kitab al-Manazir* completed ~1021 CE.

### Discovery of Magnetic Declination (1088)
Shen Kuo's *Dream Pool Essays* (*Mengxi Bitan*), 1088 CE.

### Copernican Heliocentrism (1543)
*De revolutionibus orbium coelestium* published 1543.

### Vesalius' Human Anatomy (1543)
*De Humani Corporis Fabrica* published 1543.

### Galileo's Telescopic Observations (1609)
Galileo constructed his first telescope in mid-1609 and began astronomical observations (Moon, stars) late 1609. The *Sidereus Nuncius* reporting his findings (including Jupiter's moons) was published in March 1610. Start year is 1609 — the year of the observations themselves. **Corrected from 1610.**

### Harvey's Theory of Blood Circulation (1628)
*Exercitatio Anatomica de Motu Cordis et Sanguinis in Animalibus* published 1628.

### Newton's Principia Mathematica (1687)
*Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica* published July 5, 1687.

### Invention of Calculus (1666)
Newton developed his method of fluxions ~1665–1666 (documented in *De Methodis Serierum et Fluxionum*, written ~1671 based on 1666 work). Leibniz independently developed calculus and published in 1684. Start year 1666 reflects Newton's priority date. **Corrected from 1687** — that year belongs to the Principia, which is a separate entry.

### Newton's Opticks (1704)
*Opticks* published 1704.

### Linnaean Taxonomy (1735)
*Systema Naturae* first edition published 1735.

### Franklin's Electrical Experiments (1752)
Kite experiment traditionally dated 1752.

### Lavoisier's Theory of Combustion (1789)
*Traité Élémentaire de Chimie* published 1789. Priestley isolated oxygen in 1774 and Lavoisier theorized it by 1777, but this entry specifically represents Lavoisier's systematic combustion theory as published in his 1789 treatise. The 1789 date is intentional.

### Smallpox Vaccination (1796)
Jenner's first vaccination experiment: May 14, 1796.

### Dalton's Atomic Theory (1808)
*A New System of Chemical Philosophy* Part 1 published 1808. Dalton presented the theory in lectures from 1803, but 1808 is the formal publication date.

### Avogadro's Hypothesis (1811)
Published in *Journal de Physique* 1811.

### Faraday's Electromagnetic Induction (1831)
Demonstrated experimentally in 1831.

### Cell Theory (1838)
Schleiden's cell theory for plants (1838) and Schwann's extension to animals (1839). 1838 used as start.

### Doppler Effect (1842)
Christian Doppler presented "Über das farbige Licht der Doppelsterne" in 1842.

### On the Origin of Species (1859)
Published November 24, 1859.

### Kirchhoff's Spectroscopy (1859)
Kirchhoff and Bunsen's spectral analysis papers published 1859.

### Maxwell's Equations (1865)
Full set presented in "A Dynamical Theory of the Electromagnetic Field" (1865). Earlier partial formulations appeared 1861–1862.

### Mendel's Laws of Inheritance (1866)
"Versuche über Pflanzenhybriden" published 1866; largely ignored until rediscovery in 1900.

### Mendeleev's Periodic Table (1869)
Mendeleev presented his periodic table in 1869.

### Germ Theory of Disease (1876)
Koch's anthrax work (1876) is the conventional anchor for the mature germ theory.

### Wallace-Darwin Natural Selection (1858)
Darwin-Wallace joint papers read at the Linnean Society, July 1, 1858.

### Discovery of Radio Waves (1887)
Hertz's confirming experiments: 1887.

### Discovery of X-Rays (1895)
Röntgen: November 8, 1895.

### Discovery of Radioactivity (1896)
Becquerel: 1896.

### Discovery of the Electron (1897)
J.J. Thomson: 1897.

### Discovery of Polonium and Radium (1898)
Polonium announced July 1898, radium December 1898.

### Planck's Quantum Theory (1900)
Planck's quantum hypothesis: December 14, 1900.

### Special Theory of Relativity (1905)
Einstein's paper published June 30, 1905 (Annus Mirabilis).

### Pavlov's Classical Conditioning (1906)
Pavlov's conditioning research published in Russian 1906.

### Freud's Psychoanalytic Theory (1900)
*Die Traumdeutung* published November 1899, dated 1900 by publisher.

### Bohr Model of the Atom (1913)
Bohr's trilogy of atomic structure papers published 1913.

### Continental Drift Theory (1912)
Wegener presented his theory in January 1912 (published in full 1915). 1912 is the presentation date.

### Quantum Mechanics (1925)
Heisenberg's matrix mechanics (1925) and Schrödinger's wave mechanics (1926). 1925 used as start.

### Big Bang Theory (1927)
Lemaître's expanding universe hypothesis published 1927.

### Discovery of Penicillin (1928)
Fleming's observation: September 1928.

### Hubble's Expanding Universe (1929)
Hubble's paper "A Relation between Distance and Radial Velocity among Extra-Galactic Nebulae" published 1929.

### Turing Machine and Computability (1936)
"On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem" published 1936.

### Nuclear Fission (1938)
Hahn and Strassmann's experiments: December 1938.

### DNA as Genetic Material (1944)
Avery–MacLeod–McCarty experiment published January 1944.

### Plate Tectonics Theory (1960)
Sea-floor spreading proposed by Hess ~1960 (published 1962). Vine-Matthews-Morley (1963) and widespread acceptance in the late 1960s. 1960 is used as the start of the modern framework.

### DNA Double Helix Structure (1953)
Watson and Crick's paper in *Nature*, April 25, 1953.

### Standard Model of Particle Physics (1973)
Reached substantially complete form ~1973 with QCD formulation and confirmation of quarks. Electroweak unification (Glashow-Salam-Weinberg) was 1967–1968; the Standard Model as a unified framework is conventionally dated to 1973.

### Discovery of HIV (1983)
Montagnier's group at Institut Pasteur isolated the virus in 1983.

### World Wide Web (1991)
Tim Berners-Lee made the Web publicly available in 1991. (Proposal 1989; first website December 1990.)

### Discovery of the Higgs Boson (2012)
ATLAS and CMS at CERN announced discovery July 4, 2012.

### CRISPR-Cas9 Gene Editing (2012)
Doudna and Charpentier's paper published in *Science*, June 28, 2012 (online).

### Detection of Gravitational Waves (2016)
LIGO detected the first signal September 14, 2015; announcement February 11, 2016. Start year 2016 reflects the public announcement date.

## Notes on Key Dates

Dates are stored as negative integers in years (e.g. 538.8 Ma = -538800000).

**First Life / LUCA (~3800 Ma):** The -3800000000 value is a reasonable lower bound. Microfossils from the Apex Chert (Western Australia) were long cited at ~3500 Ma; more recent evidence from the Dresser Formation and Isua greenstone belt pushes signatures of life to ~3700–3800 Ma. LUCA is not directly dated.

**First Eukaryotes (~2100 Ma):** The -2100000000 value is consistent with the oldest accepted eukaryotic microfossils (Grypania spiralis). Molecular clock estimates vary 1500–2200 Ma.

**First Multicellular Life (~600 Ma):** Ediacaran Biota. Dickinsonia and contemporaries date to ~575–560 Ma; the -600000000 value is a reasonable outer bound.

**First Fish (~530 Ma):** Myllokunmingia and Haikouichthys from the Chengjiang biota, ~518–520 Ma. The -530000000 value is a slight overestimate but within common citation range.

**First Land Plants (~470 Ma):** Spore evidence (cryptospores) extends to ~470 Ma (Ordovician). Body fossils of liverwort-like plants appear ~450 Ma. The -470000000 value reflects the spore evidence.

**First Insects (~400 Ma):** Rhyniognatha hirsti from the Rhynie Chert is ~407 Ma, making -400000000 a slight underestimate but within acceptable rounding. Some molecular clock estimates push insect origins to ~479 Ma (Ordovician), but the fossil record supports ~400–410 Ma.

**First Amphibians (~375 Ma):** Tiktaalik roseae (~375 Ma) is a fish with tetrapod-like features; Ichthyostega and Acanthostega (~365 Ma) are the first true tetrapods. The -375000000 value is consistent with Tiktaalik as the transitional form.

**First Reptiles (~312 Ma):** Hylonomus lyelli from Joggins, Nova Scotia, dates to ~312 Ma. Westlothiana lizziae (~338 Ma) was once considered earliest but is now thought to be a more basal amniote or stem reptile. -312000000 is the consensus earliest unambiguous amniote.

**First Dinosaurs (~230 Ma):** Nyasasaurus parringtoni may extend dinosaur origins to ~243 Ma (Middle Triassic), but Eoraptor (~231 Ma) and Herrerasaurus (~231 Ma) are well-established. The -230000000 value is widely cited.

**First Mammals (~225 Ma):** Adelobasileus cromptoni (~225 Ma, Late Triassic). Morganucodon dates to ~205 Ma. The -225000000 value is consistent with current evidence.

**First Birds (~150 Ma):** Archaeopteryx lithographica from the Solnhofen limestone, ~150 Ma (Tithonian, Late Jurassic). Correct.

**K-Pg Extinction (66 Ma):** ICS 2023 places the Cretaceous/Paleogene boundary at 66.0 Ma. Correct.

**Homo sapiens (~300,000 years ago):** Jebel Irhoud fossils (Morocco) date to ~315,000–286,000 years ago; -300000 is the accepted consensus value.

# Sources: pandemics.csv

## Methodology

Dates are given in years relative to the Common Era. Negative values = BCE. Start and end years represent the conventional dates for each outbreak's main wave or full duration. Many pandemics have fuzzy endpoints; the values here reflect the most widely cited scholarly range.

Primary references:
- Encyclopedia Britannica (britannica.com)
- World Health Organization historical records
- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) — pandemic histories
- Wikipedia (cross-checked against primary references above)
