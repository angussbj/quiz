# Sources: Major Technology Inventions

## Primary Reference Sources

- **Wikipedia** — used as the starting point for each entry; cross-referenced against other sources where dates were contested.
- **Encyclopaedia Britannica** — consulted for prehistoric and ancient inventions.
- **Smithsonian Magazine** — archaeology and prehistoric technology articles.
- **Nature / Science journals** — for specific dating of prehistoric items (pottery, fire, stone tools).

## Prehistoric Inventions (before 10,000 BCE)

These dates are archaeological estimates with wide uncertainty ranges. The `start_year` field reflects the current scientific consensus or conservative mainstream estimate.

| Invention | Date Used | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Stone Tools | -3300000 | Lomekwi 3 site, Kenya (2015 discovery). Oldowan tradition ~2.6 Mya is also well-documented. 3.3 Mya is the earliest confirmed date. |
| Controlled Fire | -1000000 | Conservative mainstream estimate. Evidence at Wonderwerk Cave, South Africa suggests ~1 Mya. Hearth evidence at Qesem Cave, Israel ~400,000 BCE. Some claims of 1.7 Mya (Homo erectus) are contested. |
| Bow and Arrow | -70000 | Backed microliths at Pinnacle Point and Sibudu Cave, South Africa, suggest ~70,000 BCE. Widely adopted ~30,000 BCE. The earlier date is used as the invention point. |
| Sewing Needle | -50000 | Bone needles from Denisova Cave, Siberia, ~50,000 BCE. |
| Pottery | -20000 | **Corrected from -29000.** Xianrendong Cave, China: pottery vessel shards dated ~20,000–18,000 BCE (Wu et al., 2012, *Science*). The earlier value of -29000 corresponds to fired clay figurines from Dolni Vestonice (Czech Republic), which are not pottery vessels. |

## Ancient and Classical Inventions (10,000 BCE – 500 CE)

| Invention | Date Used | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Agriculture | -10000 | Multiple independent origins ~10,000 BCE: Fertile Crescent (wheat/barley), China (rice/millet), Mesoamerica (maize). |
| Irrigation | -6000 | Early canal systems in Mesopotamia (modern Iraq) ~6000 BCE. |
| Plough | -4000 | Earliest ard (scratch plough) evidence in Mesopotamia ~4000 BCE. |
| Wheel | -3500 | Bronocice pot (Poland) and Ljubljana Marsh Wheel (Slovenia) both ~3500–3400 BCE. Mesopotamian origin most accepted. |
| Writing | -3200 | Cuneiform tablets from Uruk, Mesopotamia ~3200 BCE. Contemporaneous with Egyptian hieroglyphics. |
| Sailboat | -3200 | Depictions on Egyptian pottery ~3200 BCE. Earliest functional watercraft predate this but sailing specifically ~3200 BCE. |
| Bronze | -3300 | Early Bronze Age begins ~3300 BCE in the Aegean and Anatolia. Slightly predates the cleaner "3000 BCE" figure but well-supported archaeologically. |
| Calendar | -3000 | Babylonian and Egyptian calendars ~3000 BCE. Mesoamerican calendar systems developed independently later. |
| Papyrus | -3000 | Earliest papyrus documents from Egypt ~3000 BCE (First Dynasty). |
| Iron Smelting | -1200 | Iron Age begins ~1200 BCE in the Near East/Anatolia. Scattered earlier iron use (meteoritic iron) is not smelting. |
| Alphabet | -1050 | Phoenician alphabet ~1050 BCE, the ancestor of most modern alphabets. Earlier proto-alphabetic scripts (~1800 BCE) are sometimes cited but the Phoenician system is the decisive point. |
| Aqueduct | -800 | Early Assyrian water channels predate the Roman aqueducts. The Aqueduct of Jerwan (Assyria) dates to ~700 BCE. -800 is a slightly conservative estimate for early enclosed water channels. Note: the Roman aqueduct (Aqua Appia, 312 BCE) is the most famous but not the first. |
| Compass | -200 | South-pointing lodestone "spoon" from Han dynasty China ~200 BCE. Navigational magnetic compass ~1040 CE (Song dynasty). The -200 date reflects the first magnetic orientation device. |
| Paper | 105 | Cai Lun's standardised papermaking process presented to the Han Emperor in 105 CE. Earlier papermaking in China existed ~200 BCE but Cai Lun's method became the widespread standard. |
| Stirrup | 300 | Early stirrup evidence in India ~200 BCE (toe stirrups); rigid stirrups in China ~300 CE. The rigid stirrup enabling cavalry combat is the significant development. |

## Medieval and Early Modern (500 CE – 1800 CE)

| Invention | Date Used | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Gunpowder | 850 | Chinese alchemical texts describing the formula ~850 CE (Tang dynasty). Earliest confirmed formula in the *Zhenyuan miaodao yaolue* text. |
| Printing Press | 1440 | Gutenberg's movable type press, Mainz, Germany, ~1440 CE. Woodblock printing existed in China ~700 CE but Gutenberg's press is the transformative event for Western history. |
| Microscope | 1590 | Zacharias Janssen (Netherlands) ~1590, though attribution is contested. Hans Lippershey also credited. Compound microscope functional by ~1600. |
| Telescope | 1608 | Hans Lippershey's patent application, Netherlands, 1608. Galileo improved and popularised it 1609–1610. |
| Steam Engine | 1769 | James Watt's separate condenser steam engine patent, 1769. Thomas Newcomen's earlier atmospheric engine (1712) is a precursor but Watt's version enabled industrial applications. |
| Smallpox Vaccine | 1796 | Edward Jenner's cowpox inoculation experiment, 1796. |
| Cotton Gin | 1793 | Eli Whitney patent, 1793. |

## Industrial and Modern Era (1800 CE – present)

| Invention | Date Used | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Electric Battery | 1800 | Alessandro Volta's voltaic pile, 1800. First device to produce a sustained electric current. |
| Steam Locomotive | 1804 | Richard Trevithick's Penydarren locomotive, 1804. First steam-powered railway locomotive. |
| Photography | 1826 | Joseph Nicéphore Niépce's "View from the Window at Le Gras," 1826–1827 — the oldest surviving photograph. Louis Daguerre's more practical daguerreotype process, 1839, is often cited but Niépce's 1826 image is earlier. |
| Telegraph | 1837 | Cooke and Wheatstone's electric telegraph patent (UK) and Morse's system both 1837. |
| Anaesthesia | 1846 | William Morton's public demonstration of ether anaesthesia, Massachusetts General Hospital, 16 October 1846. |
| Sewing Machine | 1846 | Elias Howe's patent, 1846. Earlier machines existed (Thimonnier, 1830) but Howe's lockstitch design was the basis for modern machines. |
| Germ Theory | 1861 | Louis Pasteur's published germ theory of fermentation and disease, 1861. Robert Koch's later work (1876+) confirmed and extended it. |
| Dynamite | 1867 | Alfred Nobel's patent, 1867. |
| Telephone | 1876 | Alexander Graham Bell's patent, 2 March 1876. Elisha Gray filed the same day; Bell is the legally recognised inventor. |
| Internal Combustion Engine | 1876 | Nikolaus Otto's four-stroke engine patent, 1876 (Otto cycle). Earlier two-stroke designs existed (Lenoir, 1860). |
| Electric Light Bulb | 1879 | Thomas Edison's practical incandescent bulb with carbonised bamboo filament, October 1879. Joseph Swan demonstrated a similar device in the UK around the same time. |
| Automobile | 1885 | Karl Benz's Patent-Motorwagen, 1885 — the first purpose-built petrol-powered vehicle. |
| Radio | 1895 | Guglielmo Marconi's first radio transmission demonstration, 1895. Nikola Tesla's competing claims are acknowledged but Marconi received the first patent. |
| X-Ray | 1895 | Wilhelm Röntgen's discovery, 8 November 1895. |
| Airplane | 1903 | Wright Brothers' first powered, controlled flight, Kitty Hawk, 17 December 1903. |
| Television | 1926 | John Logie Baird's first public demonstration of a working television system, London, January 1926. Electronic television (Farnsworth) followed in 1927. |
| Penicillin | 1928 | Alexander Fleming's observation of penicillin mould, September 1928. Therapeutic development by Florey and Chain came 1940–1942. |
| Nuclear Fission | 1938 | Otto Hahn and Fritz Strassmann's discovery of nuclear fission, December 1938 (published January 1939, explained by Meitner and Frisch). First nuclear weapon (Trinity test) 1945. |
| Transistor | 1947 | Bardeen, Brattain, and Shockley at Bell Labs, December 1947. Nobel Prize awarded 1956. |
| DNA Double Helix | 1953 | Watson and Crick's model published in *Nature*, April 1953, based substantially on Franklin and Wilkins' X-ray data. |
| Integrated Circuit | 1958 | Jack Kilby at Texas Instruments demonstrated the first working integrated circuit, September 1958. Robert Noyce independently invented a more practical version at Fairchild, 1959. |
| Internet | 1969 | ARPANET first message sent between UCLA and Stanford, 29 October 1969. |
| World Wide Web | 1991 | Tim Berners-Lee launched the first public website at CERN, 6 August 1991. Proposal written 1989; HTTP/HTML specifications 1990. |

## Contested or Ambiguous Dates

- **Pottery (-20000)**: Corrected from -29000 in this file. The -29000 figure cited fired clay figurines (Dolni Vestonice), not pottery vessels. The Xianrendong Cave evidence (Wu et al., 2012) is the current scientific consensus for pottery vessels.
- **Bronze (-3300)**: Some sources use -3000 as the round figure. -3300 is used here as it aligns with Early Bronze Age archaeological periodisation.
- **Paper (105)**: Papermaking existed earlier (~200 BCE) but Cai Lun's 105 CE standardisation is the conventional date. The 105 figure is used.
- **Compass (-200)**: The lodestone device (~200 BCE) is used rather than the navigational compass (~1040 CE) since the question is about the invention of the compass as an instrument.
- **Aqueduct (-800)**: The alternates label mentions "Roman aqueduct" but the date predates Rome's Aqua Appia (312 BCE). This reflects earlier Assyrian water channels. The label alternates could be improved but the date is defensible.
